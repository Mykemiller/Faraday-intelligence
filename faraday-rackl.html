Power Architecture

Hyperscalers accelerating 800V DC transition as Blackwell rack densities push 120kW per cabinet

OCP's latest working group update confirms three Big 5 operators are moving forward with 800V DC distribution in new campus builds, bypassing AC entirely. NFPA code gap remains the constraint for retrofit projects.

Faraday's Take

The code gap is the bottleneck, not the technology. Whoever moves on NFPA first shapes the retrofit market. Watch Schneider and Eaton's lobbying posture at NFPA 70 hearings — that's where the real signal is.

SOURCE: OCP Working Group Update · March 31, 2026
Grid & Regulatory

14th state introduces moratorium legislation as PJM queue hits 3,400 pending interconnection requests

Good Jobs First tracker updated overnight. Virginia and Ohio remain open; Pennsylvania legislation advances to committee vote. PJM's latest load forecast shows 47 GW of data center demand by 2030 — up 18% from February guidance.

Faraday's Take

The moratorium wave is reshaping site selection faster than any operator anticipated. Entitled, powered land in non-moratorium states is now the scarcest asset in the market. Expect land premiums to follow.

SOURCE: Good Jobs First · PJM Load Forecast Q1 2026

DigitalBridge closes B SoftBank deal as PE-backed development pipeline surpasses B globally

Chips & Density

Microsoft confirms first Vera Rubin NVL72 power-on at Wisconsin campus — density at 132kW per rack

Datacenter Dynamics reports Microsoft as first hyperscaler to bring Vera Rubin NVL72 to operational status. The 132kW figure confirms density projections at the upper end of estimates. Liquid cooling infrastructure was staged six months prior to deployment.

Faraday's Take

132kW per rack is not a data point — it's a forcing function. Every new campus design needs to price this in today. Air cooling's window is closing faster than the market is building to replace it.

SOURCE: Datacenter Dynamics · LinkedIn Intel · March 31, 2026
